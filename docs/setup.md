# Setup walkthrough

The friendly, step-by-step version. If you just want the fast path, copy the box in [PROMPT.md](../PROMPT.md) and paste it into Claude Code. This page is for when you want a little more hand-holding.

## What you need

1. **Claude Code** ([install](https://www.anthropic.com/claude-code)) is the AI that reads your folder and builds everything. It can see and edit files on your computer, which is what makes the automatic setup possible.
2. **Obsidian** ([obsidian.md](https://obsidian.md), free) is optional. It gives you a nice editor and the graph view, but your notes are plain text either way. You can add it later.

That is the whole stack. Two tools, and one is optional.

## Step 1: pick a home

Decide roughly where you want your second brain to live. A folder in your Documents is fine. If you already use Obsidian, you can use your existing vault. You do not have to create anything by hand, the prompt will find your notes or help you make a folder.

## Step 2: open Claude Code there

Open Claude Code in (or near) that folder, so it can see your files. If you are not sure, just open it in your home folder and tell it where to look when it asks.

## Step 3: paste the prompt

Copy everything inside the box in [PROMPT.md](../PROMPT.md) and paste it in. Claude will say something like *"Let's build your personalized second brain. I'll ask a few quick questions, then set it all up."*

## Step 4: answer the questions

It asks a handful, one at a time. There are no wrong answers:

- **Where your notes live** (it will try to find them first).
- **Your operating system** (so paths and scheduling are right).
- **Whether you dictate notes** (so it handles transcripts carefully).
- **What you want to put in** (emails, documents, notes, transcripts, blogs, anything you have written).
- **Anything to keep private** (a folder it will never read).
- **How often to summarize** (daily, weekly, monthly).
- **Whether you want a dream cycle**, and whether it should run on a schedule or on command.

## Step 5: confirm, and let it build

It shows you a short plan. You say go. It creates the folders, writes the `CLAUDE.md` rules, sets up your templates and commands, and (if you chose it) schedules the summaries. Then it offers to start your first daily note.

## Using it day to day

- **Capture:** say `start today's note` and talk or type. Do not organize as you go, just get it down.
- **Bring things in:** point it at a pile of files and say `import this`.
- **Reflect:** once a week, say `summarize my week`.
- **Consolidate:** once you have a few weeks, say `run a dream cycle` and approve what it surfaces.

The more you put in, the smarter it gets about you. Start simple. You can always go [deeper](customize.md) later.
